Frequently Asked Questions

Everything You
Need to Know

What is a baraat and how does it work?

+
The baraat is the groom's wedding procession — one of the most joyful and celebrated moments in a South Asian wedding. Traditionally, the groom rides atop the horse while family and friends dance, play music, and celebrate around him. A horse-drawn carriage is also available as an alternative for families who prefer it.

What areas of Texas do you serve?

+
We serve the entire state of Texas. Our primary baraat service areas include Houston, Dallas-Fort Worth, Austin, and San Antonio, along with surrounding communities including Sugar Land, Katy, Plano, Frisco, Irving, McKinney, Georgetown, Round Rock, and Fredericksburg. We travel statewide — our horse and carriage come to you on a trailer. Contact us with your venue location and we will confirm availability.

Can we decorate the horse and carriage?

+
Absolutely. We welcome marigold garlands, floral arrangements, fabric draping, and any decorations meaningful to the family and the wedding's aesthetic. We encourage families to provide their own decorations — passed to us at setup through the wedding coordinator — so the result reflects exactly the vision for the day.

How far in advance should we book?

+
As early as possible — ideally 3 to 6 months before your wedding date. Spring and fall wedding weekends fill up quickly across Houston, Dallas, and Austin. Custom decoration requests require more lead time. Contact us as soon as your date is confirmed.

Are your horses calm with dhol and dancing?

+
Yes. Our horses are experienced in high-energy, celebratory environments with music, dhol drums, crowds, and dancing. Calm and completely at ease, no matter what the celebration brings.

Do you also provide carriages for the vidaai?

+
Yes. We provide horse-drawn carriages for the vidaai — the bride's beautiful send-off as she begins her new life. We can coordinate both the baraat and vidaai as a full-day package.
